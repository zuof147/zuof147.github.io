"The City's Observer"

Image Collector: Emily Yi
Web Designer: Sophia Zuo

Bacially, the photo collection this website applys is an archive of street photography of people, events, etc. shot on color negatives and black and white film in NYC.

When I first see it, I thought that these collections of photograph definitely have something really interesting going on.

The context is clearly the 1940s-1970s of America. For the United States, 1945–1964 was a time of high economic growth and general prosperity. It was also a time of confrontation as the capitalist United States and its allies politically opposed the Soviet Union and other communist states; the Cold War had begun. African Americans united and organized, and a triumph of the Civil Rights Movement ended Jim Crow segregation in the Southern United States. Further laws were passed that made discrimination illegal and provided federal oversight to guarantee voting rights.

The story was happening at all type of places, it is all about people’s lives, how they lived in this society. Different individuals with different personalities were gathered in this city and they all got their own thing to do, or interact with each other like talking, leaning on the wall to wait for somebody, set besides the road to play poker, or even fighting with bicycles holding in their hands.

These photographs all have backgrounds, some are the streets, subways and the cars parked beside the road to show people’s activities in the city.

They are photographed with maybe vintage cameras. Because they are old photos, some of the photos are not of high resolution but it also shows the sense of the age. So I also play with the timeline in my project and add blurry filters to emphasize the sense of age.
